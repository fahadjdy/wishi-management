Step 3 · Rules

How winners are picked & who can join

Winner selection style

Random or Hybrid — the two WISHI types, explained on the next slide.

Who picks the winner?

Automatic — the system decides (a fair draw, or the lowest bid).   Manual — the organiser chooses an eligible member, and the reason is recorded.

Join rules

  • Require approval — join requests wait in the organiser's queue for an OK. Recommended.
  • Auto-join link — anyone with the private link joins instantly, skipping approval.

The two types

Types of WISHIChosen at creation — it sets how each cycle's winner is decided.

Type 1

Random WISHI

Every cycle's winner is chosen by a fair, verifiable random draw. The simplest and most popular style — no bidding involved.

  • Everyone has an equal chance each cycle, until it's their turn.
  • The draw is recorded, so the result can't be disputed.
  • Best for friends & family groups who just want it fair.
Type 2

Hybrid WISHI

A custom mix of Random cycles and Tender (bidding) cycles. The organiser sets the pattern and it repeats across the WISHI.

  • Example pattern: Random → Random → Tender, then repeat.
  • Some cycles are a lucky draw; others are open to bidding.
  • Best when the group wants flexibility, like a traditional chit fund.

Inside a cycle

Two ways a cycle can run

Random cycle

The winner is drawn by luck from the members who haven't won yet. Quick, simple, and completely fair.

Tender (Bidding) cycle

Members bid for an early turn. Whoever is willing to take the least wins the cycle — and the amount they give up (the “discount”) is shared back with the whole group. This is the classic chit-fund auction.

Who makes the final call?
Automatic — the system picks the winner (the random draw, or the lowest bid).  •  Manual — the organiser selects an eligible member themselves, and the reason is logged for transparency.
